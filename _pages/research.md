My research interests are primarily in L2 acquisition of constructions in Chinese, corpus linguistics, and sociolinguistics. I have been working on how L2 learners of Chinese acquire the bei passive as a form-meaning pair within the framework of construction grammar. Also, I am interested in how L2 learners of Chinese acquire and use pragmatic markers in Chiense, such as utterance-final particles.

* L2 Acquisition of Constructions in Chinese (work with Dr. Feng-hsi Liu)
In construction grammar, a construction is defined as a form-meaning pair. When applied the theory to L2 acquisition, it predicts that the form-meaning pair can be learned. In Chinese, the bei passive construction is often associated with adversative meaning. The question therefore arises whether L2 learners of Chinese are able to acquire its form as well as its meaning. Our findings show that both low-level and high-level learners are able to associate its meaning with its form. However, high-level learners make the association independent of the meaning of the verb, while low-level learners are still on their way to associate the meaning with the construction itself.

    -Chen, Chen and Feng-hsi Liu. “L2 Acquisition of the bei Passive in Mandarin Chinese—A Constructionist Approach.” To appear. Chinese as a Second Language Research.

* Chinese Learner Corpora (work with Yuwei Wang)
In this collaborative project we (PI: Chen Chen; co-PI: Yuwei Wang) aim to build a longitudianl learner corpus and finally an online platform of teachers’ instructions and their students’ speech and writing from first and target language contexts.
