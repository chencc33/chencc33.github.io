My research interests are primarily in L2 acquisition of form-meaning pairs, corpus linguistics, and sociolinguistics. 

**L2 Acquisition of Form-Meaning Pairs in Chinese**

My research in this area has focused on *bei* passive constructions, constructions that contain *gei*, and the aspect marker *le*. By examining L2 construction learning through experimental and corpus linguistic approaches, my findings show that L2 learners of Chinese are able to acquire linguistic forms as well as semantic meanings. My publication/presentations in this area include: 

**Chen, C**. & Liu, F. H. (2020). L2 acquisition of the *bei* passive in Mandarin Chinese: A constructionist approach. Chinese as a Second Language Research, 9(2), 169­198. DOI: [https://doi.org/10.1515/caslar­2020­0007](https://doi.org/10.1515/caslar­2020­0007).

**Chen, C**.(2019, March). *Construction-­based Approach to L2 Acquisition of the *Bei* passive in Chinese*. Paper presented at the annual meeting of the American Association of Applied Linguistics (AAAL). Atlanta, GA. 


**Corpus Linguistics and Utterance-final Particles**

I am particularly interested in applying corpus linguistic approaches and computational techniques to L2 research of pragmatics and syntactic structures. I have been collaborating with Dr. Wenhao Diao to examine L1 and L2 use of utterance-final particles in the [dorm talk corpus](https://dormtalk.arizona.edu/). 

Diao, W. & **Chen, C**. (2021). L2 use of pragmatic markers in peer talk: Mandarin utterance final particles. *International Review of Applied Linguistics in Language Teaching*, (), 000010151520200148. DOI: [https://doi.org/10.1515/iral­2020­0148](https://doi.org/10.1515/iral­2020­0148) 

Diao, W. & **Chen, C**. (Forthcoming). *Softening the tone?: A corpus­-based study of the utterance­-final pragmatic particle ba in Mandarin*. In L. Shuai (Eds.), Pragmatics of Chinese as a Second Language. Multilingual Matters. 

**Chen, C**., Diao, W. (2021, July). *Talk in the dorm: A corpus­-based study of the utterance final pragmatic marker BA in Mandarin by L1 and L2 speakers*. Paper presented on the panel Contrastive Corpus Approaches to Pragmatic Markers: Variation in Use, Translation Issues and Pedagogical Applications (organized by G. Anderson, S. Gesuato, M. T. Musacchio) at the 17th International Pragmatics Conference (IPrA). Winterthur, Switzerland. 

**Chen, C**., & Diao, W. (2021, March). *A corpus-­based study of L2 pragmatic markers in study abroad: Mandarin utterance­-final particles*. Paper presented at the annual meeting of the American Association of Applied Linguistics (AAAL), virtual meeting. 


**Building a Longitudinal Chinese Learner Corpus**

I am currently collaborating with Yuwei Wang to build a longitudinal learner corpus of students’ speech and writing productions and, finally, an online platform. This project is funded by the College of Humanities Graduate Research Grant. To date, we have collected learner data for one and half a year and have transcribed more than 100,000 Chinese tokens. 

**Chen, C**., & Wang, Y. (2021, March). *Learner Corpus: Powerful Tool for L2 Research and Teaching*. Talk given at the East Asian Brown Bag Lecture Series, University of Arizona.
