Chen Chen is a Ph.D. student in Chinese Linguistics at the Department of East Asian Studies, University of Arizona. Her research interests include L2 acquisition of Chinese, corpus linguistics, and sociolinguistics. She has worked on L2 acquisition of the bei passive construction, aspect-marker -le, and utterance-final particles. She has presented her work at a number of important conferences, including American Association for Applied Linguistics, Second language Acquisition Research Forum and Chinese Language Teachers Associations. 

Her research continuously inform her teaching practice. For the past five years, she has been involved in diverse teaching contexts, including intensive summer language program (Princeton in Beijing), college-level langauge courses, and K-12 Chinese classes. She is good at creating real-life contexts with multimedia resources to help students appropriately use linguistic structures and achieve the communicative objectives. Especially, she pays more attention to the meaning/function part of each construction form, such as the negative meaning often associated with the bei passives and the pragmatic functions of utterance-final particles.
